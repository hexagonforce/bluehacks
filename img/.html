The Groupmate Theory: Weird and the Normal (Part 2)

15 Oct 2017

Hi. This is the second part of my translation of the wonderful “Groupmates” section of the Namu Wiki article “Group Projects”. This one is going to talk about the Weird and the Normal types of groupmates which you would almost always encounter.

Esoteric but not necessarily bad

The Lazy Genius

The case when a person is waaay beyond competent but are very lazy.

These people usually do something else when others debate and work with the project. So people who don’t recognize a Lazy Genius might view him or her as freeloaders or plainly lazy people. Even when he/she is reporting he’s very lax and chill, so other members might question the attitude of this guy.

Of course, their abilities show through their results. Doesn’t show up to meetings, looks at the presentations on the day itself, presents based on background knowledge, gets A+. In debates, they just take a look at the topic and completely wrecks the other side, or solves a problem that three people worked on for 30 minutes with one suggestion.

For these people, their concept of “common knowledge” is different from the normal “common knowledge”. For other people, these things are really difficult things that needs to be researched for hours on the internet. But for these people, it’s “Do I necessarily have to research this?” so they find prep time for presentations a waste. When they don’t pay attention to meetings, they’re basically saying “LOL what an easy problem, what’s the matter with them…”

Since the results are good no one can really attack their lack of participation. He can just say “dude I carried and we got a good score right?” and no one can really say anything.

To use these people well, give them two peoples’ worth of work and give him an “assistant” who’s job it is to ‘do the dirty work’ and ‘to keep the Lazy Genius in check’. Then they can show great ability better than a well trained corporal in the military. Do not make lazy geniuses leaders because leaders have to take care of a lot of menial things but these people are lazy.

Members with Connections

These people know other people who are experts at their majors. This happens more often in Archi or Engineeringg courses or the Arts. These people can just ask for materials from their connections so people w/o connections would obviously be at a disadvantage. It feels very unfair when this type of member says “That’s all you can do?”, “You gotta try harder”, or “Can you please get out of our group?” just because you don’t have connections. Yeah, they got connections, but the question is do they have actual skill?. And it’s bearable if the member actually got good materials, but if he/she keeps trash-talking but the results are bad…

In law or business sometimes the prof asks you to interview lawyers or high-ranking officials from big companies. It’s like they’re testing your connections and not your ability. Even if you say that you don’t have connections, the prof would just cut with “Try harder, if you try hard enough you’d find one”. So the tasks ends with either paying a lawyer in the neighborhood or getting a bad grade. This happens because the prof’s standard of his friends makes him think that “You’d know at least one lawyer or officer in your life”. Of course, that’s not true for most other people. However, if your parents have connections then you can do these things well enough.

Normal members

Half-forced Solo-Carry

Unlike voluntary solo-carries, these people were forced to solo-carry a group project. Most people who complain about group works are in this type.

  • If there’s a 5 member group project but two people withdrew the class. If it’s only the first few days of the work, you could move somewhere else; however, if it’s already halfway through you’re forced to carry on with lacking members.
  • Due to circumstance or personality, other members quietly force the one person to solo-carry. Of course, the quality of the work would deteriorate in this case because the carry was forced to carry. Unfortunately, most professors don’t actually check participation in the groups, and peer reviewing will always end up with high scores for everyone (…) therefore it really sucks to be a half-forced Solo-carry.

The quiet member

This type of member aren’t very active, but they receive orders and do them well. Although there are quiet members who are actually good at their work, some of them are just hard-working without much ability(…). At least these types are better than freeloaders. But they have little to no impact in the group.

Most quiet members produce decent output unless very unlucky.

Me, Myself, and I

This type of members are similar to Solo-Carries but they never do more than their assigned work. They might be good at their work, but they refuse anyone who tries to give them more work. This is why they tend to be viewed as uncooperative, and even if they do well, if the rest of the members suck, the entire thing collapses. At least they’re not freeloaders.

But if everyone in a group is of this type, that group fails. No exceptions.

Everyone else is too good for me

This situation (not really a type) occurs when good and bad groupmates are obviously defined. Say there are two 4th year cum laude students and some normal 1st year students. The latter will never reach the standards of the former. These people are sometimes relegated to doing menial tasks but that might as well be the best choice because when this type of groupmate is given important work, either the work turns out to be bad or the person in question crumbles to pressure.
